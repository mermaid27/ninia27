What is Written Translation?

Written translation is the translation of written resources into the desired language and their redaction in the most general sense. All written sources such as documents, contracts, agreements, literary texts, correspondence, e-mails are types of written translations. Communication between individuals, societies and organizations can be achieved by using written translations. Written translations play an important role in the introduction of companies to the outside world, the announcement of activities in the digital environment to the world, reporting and being able to follow the world agenda within the scope of international trade. The level of education and specialization of translators are very important for a healthy and easy communication. Professional written translation service is a service that is needed in many areas ranging from diplomacy to trade, law to art and science. It is very important to transfer the information correctly and provide professional translation throughout the written translation process. In case of any translation error, there may be incorrect information transfer which may cause legal problems in official documents. ADH Avrasya Translation continues to work meticulously with its expert translation team in order to help you not encounter such situations. It offers a secure written translation service in all languages apart from highly demanded languages such as English written translation, German written translation, French written translation.

What are the Types of Written Translation?

Since the written translation is needed in all areas, it is divided into fields of expertise and types in order to make the translation properly. A translator who specializes in the field of law can better translate the translation of legal written text, while the translation of a literary text should be translated by interpreters who are experts in literary texts. Some of the types of written translations and expertise fields are as follows.

  1. Written Translation of Commercial Texts
  2. Technical Text Translation (Academic Translation)
  3. Social Sciences and Human Sciences Translation
  4. Literary Translation
  5. Legal & Law
  6. Medical Translation
  7. Finance Translation
  8. Notary Certified Translation
  9. Agreements
  10. Contracts.
